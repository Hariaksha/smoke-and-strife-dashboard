Fire-driven haze exposure and social conflict in Indonesia, re-estimated weekly with an upwind-fire instrument on updating ACLED conflict data, NASA FIRMS/VIIRS fire detections, and ERA5 winds. Companion to the working paper by Hariaksha Gunda (University of Alabama).

Effect of fire intensity on conflict, by conflict-activity threshold

IV coefficient on instrumented log FRP with district and year-month fixed effects, province-clustered standard errors, and one distributed lag. Each row restricts the panel to districts with conflict in at least τ of sample months — the paper's headline gradient: a null on average, increasingly negative where conflict is a recurring phenomenon. Whiskers are 95% confidence intervals.

Which kinds of conflict respond? Event-type decomposition (τ ≥ 30% sample)

The suppression effect is concentrated in riots and violence against civilians; planned protests and organized battles are unaffected — the pattern that distinguishes a spontaneous-crowd mechanism from a simple disruption-of-assembly story.
